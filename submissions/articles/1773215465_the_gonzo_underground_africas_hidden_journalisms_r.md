# The Gonzo Underground: Africa's Hidden Journalisms Rising

Africa doesn't have gonzo journalists. It has journalists who survive.

In a continent where press freedom ranks among the world's most dangerous, a new generation of immersive storytellers are going further than Hunter S. Thompson ever imagined—they embed, they infiltrate, they disappear, and some never come back.

---

## ANAS ARMEYAW ANAS (Ghana)

The man who never shows his face.

Disguised as a madman, a woman, a Sheikh—he exposed 34 judges taking bribes, brought down Ghana's entire football association, and had his collaborator assassinated in 2019.

That's not gonzo tourism. That's gonzo warfare.

He wears masks. He uses hidden cameras. He becomes the story while becoming invisible. The reporter isn't the story—the truth is the story. The reporter is just the vessel.

## FISAYO SOYOMBO (Nigeria)

Got himself arrested—actually arrested—to expose police corruption.

Spent 13 days in a Lagos cell and Ikoyi Prison. That's gonzo: living the story from inside a jail cell.

He didn't just observe the corruption. He *became* the detainee. He documented everything. Then he published it.

## THE ANONYMOUS UGANDAN

One journalist went so deep undercover that she was sold into slavery.

Literally sold.

To investigate domestic worker trafficking from Uganda to Dubai, she posed as a maid-seeking woman, got recruited, and was purchased by a family. She prayed for someone to buy her so she could expose the trafficking ring.

She was bought. She investigated. She escaped. She exposed.

We don't know her name. Her anonymity is her protection.
