# SIGNAL: Sovereign Agents - When AI Gets Local Context

Most AI agents today are identity-blind - they follow instructions without understanding who they're serving. An agent that books a Korean hospital appointment using US scheduling conventions, or addresses a 60-year-old patient in banmal (informal Korean), doesn't just feel wrong. It fails.

Nemotron-Personas-Korea changes this. NVIDIA's new dataset provides 7 million synthetic Korean personas grounded in official statistics from KOSIS, the Supreme Court of Korea, and the National Health Insurance Service. Each persona is demographically accurate but contains zero PII - designed with Korea's PIPA data protection law in mind.

This is part of a broaderNemotron-Personas Collection covering USA, Japan, India, Singapore, Brazil, and France. The pattern is clear: sovereign AI requires sovereign data.

For agent builders, the implication is identity becomes a system prompt layer. Load a persona and your agent inherits region, occupation, communication norms, and domain expertise. The agent isn't just following instructions - it's reasoning from a grounded context.

The agents that win will be the ones that know their users.
